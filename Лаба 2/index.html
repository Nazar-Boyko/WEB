The Animals

The Animals were a British rock band formed in Newcastle upon Tyne in the early 1960s. The group achieved international fame and success in the mid-1960s, thanks to their bluesy and R&B-inspired rock music. Their most notable hit was "The House of the Rising Sun," which topped the charts in the United Kingdom and the United States in 1964.The band consisted of Eric Burdon (vocals), Alan Price (keyboard), Hilton Valentine (guitar), Chas Chandler (bass), and John Steel (drums). Their music was characterized by powerful vocals from Burdon and a distinctive organ sound by Price.

The Animals' career was relatively short-lived, and they went through several lineup changes. After their initial success, Price left the band, and the group continued to produce hits such as "Don't Let Me Be Misunderstood" and "We Gotta Get Out of This Place." The Animals disbanded in the mid-1960s, and various members pursued solo careers and other musical endeavors. However, they left a lasting impact on the rock music scene of the time and are remembered as one of the key bands in the British Invasion era. Their music continues to be celebrated and influential in the rock and blues genres.
